# SOCIAL CAUSALITY RULES

Rules for Phase1-D UX intelligence copy.

## Acceptable reasoning

- **Behavior → effect on user:** "When replies stack up, you answer to lower tension, and fatigue follows."
- **Pattern naming:** "Reassurance loop", "response pressure", "attention asymmetry."
- **Conditional humility:** "may", "often", "tends to", "in this shape of interaction."
- **Actionable pacing:** widen reply windows, mute groups, pause before explaining.
- **Target-specific framing** when target profile exists.

## Forbidden reasoning

- Therapy lexicon: trauma bonding, attachment style as verdict, inner child, healing journey.
- Diagnosis: disorder names, clinical labels, "you are depressed/anxious."
- Personality verdicts: insecure, narcissist, toxic personality, MBTI as identity.
- Fake certainty: "will definitely leave you", "proof that they don't love you."
- Generic templates with no causal link: "great compatibility vibes", "trust the universe."

## Specificity rules

1. Tie each sentence to **one interaction mechanism** (pace, asymmetry, loop, comparison frame).
2. Prefer **one concrete micro-scenario** on match cards (e.g. delayed reply without guilt).
3. Max **three** pressure bullets on insight; max **one** fox block.
4. Avoid repeating the same pattern name in adjacent lines.

## Anti-template rules

- Ban standalone labels without mechanism: "情緒壓力：中度" as the only explanation.
- Replace "相容度：高" vibes with **why interaction feels lighter**.
- Match cards must include: lighter interaction, reduced pressure, rhythm, fatigue avoided, one scenario.
